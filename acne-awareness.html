Card 01 · Cover

What acne
actually is.

Acne happens when a pore, the tiny opening around a hair follicle, gets blocked. Your skin makes sebum, a natural oil that keeps it supple. When too much sebum meets dead skin cells and a strain of bacteria that lives on everyone's skin (C. acnes), the pore can clog, swell, and become the spot you see in the mirror.

It is not a hygiene problem. It is not a moral failing. Most of the drivers are inside your body, not on your pillowcase. Knowing your triggers is the first step to calming them.

Card 02 · The list

Five real drivers,
translated.

Hormones, sebum, clogged pores, bacteria, and genes. Plus a handful of things that make acne worse but rarely cause it on their own.

i HormonesThe big driver

Androgen hormones rise during puberty and shift around your monthly cycle. They signal your oil glands to make more sebum, which is why breakouts flare at these times.

ii SebumOil overload

Your skin's natural oil, made to keep it supple. When the glands make too much of it, the pore has more to deal with, and the cycle begins.

iii Clogged poresTrapped dead skin

Dead skin cells that should shed get sticky and block the opening. Behind the blockage, sebum and bacteria build up quietly.

iv BacteriaC. acnes

A strain that lives on everyone's skin. In a clogged pore with no air, it multiplies, and the redness and swelling you feel is your immune system showing up to fight it.

v GenesYour baseline

How oily your skin is, how sensitive your pores are to hormones, and how easily you scar are partly written in your family. None of this is your fault, but knowing it explains why your best friend's routine may not work for you.

♡

It is rarely just one thing, my love. Most acne comes from a mix of these, and most of them are not your fault. Knowing your triggers is the first step to calming them.

Card 03 · The takeaway

It is rarely
just one thing.

5 real drivers, plus the aggravators.

Beyond the five main drivers, a handful of things can worsen acne without causing it on their own: high-sugar and refined-carb foods (they spike insulin, which nudges oil production), stress, certain medications, picking at spots, and pore-clogging makeup or hair products. You do not need to cut everything out. Just notice what your skin responds to, and keep things balanced.
